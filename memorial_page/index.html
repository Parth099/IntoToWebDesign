Leonhard Euler: Famous Mathematician


Here's a time line of Euler's life:

  • 1707 - Leonhard Euler was in Basel, Switzerland
  • 1720 - enrolled at the University of Basel
  • 1723 - Graduated with a Master of Philosophy
  • 1727 - Euler joins the Imperial Russian Academy of Sciences
  • 1731 - Climbs the ranks of the Academy and becomes the professor of physics
  • 1734 - Married Katharina Gsell (1707–1773). Only 5 of their children survived to adulthood
  • 1741 - Joins the Berlin Acedemy in Germany
  • 1748 - Publishes "Introductio in analysin infinitorum", a text on functions for which he is famous for
  • 1755 - Publishes "Institutiones calculi differentialis" a paper on differential calculus. This is also the same year he is elected a foreign member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.
  • 1766 - Eulers eyesight had been declining for years now but a failed surgery leaves him nearly blind. Regardless of this Euler contined to publish many papers due to his impressive memory and mental capacity.
  • 1766 - He returnes to Russia due to war and the destruction of his estate in Germany.
  • 1970 - receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1776 - Three years after his wife's death, Euler married her half-sister, Salome Abigail Gsell (1723–1794).
  • 1783 - Suffers from a brain hemorrhage and dies hours later (Aged 76) University
