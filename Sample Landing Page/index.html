Features

Highly Electronegative

On the Pauling Scale Oxygen is a 3.5, the 2nd highest Electronegative element, only 2nd to Florine. This allows oxygen to easily attract electrons making better bonds and reactions. Moreover, these bonds are stonger due to higher attraction and the ablity to double bond.


Biological Important

Oxygen plays in important role in Photosynthesis, arguably the most important process on Earth besides the Software Development Life Cycle. Moreover the oxygen partial pressure (presure of oxygen with respect to total pressure) in any living vertebrate organism is the highest in the respiratory system impying Oxygen is used to carry out chemical reactions required for life.


Commercial Uses

Stored Oxygen can be used in tanks to go place where Oxygen is sparce like Space-exploration or under-water expeditions where free Oxygen (aerated) doesn't exist Additionally, people who climb mountains or fly airplace also carry oxygen since it harder to breathe air when air pressure is low at higher altitudes. Lastly, Oxygen is combined with metals like iron to create Steel which is lightweight and stronger. Oxygen is used to make the bonds between Iron molecules tighter so the Steel is less prone to breaks.
